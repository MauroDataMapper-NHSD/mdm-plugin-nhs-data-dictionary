<p>The     <a href="te:NHS Business Definitions|tm:ABO System">ABO System</a> is a     <a href="dm:Classes and Attributes|dc:CODED CLINICAL ENTRY">CODED CLINICAL ENTRY</a>.  </p>  <p>The     <a href="te:NHS Business Definitions|tm:ABO System">ABO System</a> is a system of some basic types into which human blood may be classified according to the presence or absence of particular antigens.  </p>  <p>The 4 types are:</p>  <ul>    <li>      <div>A - Blood group A has A antigens in its red blood cells and anti-B antibodies in its plasma;</div>    </li>    <li>      <div>B - Blood group B has B antigens and anti-A antibodies in its plasma;</div>    </li>    <li>      <div>O - Blood group O blood has no antigens but both anti-A and anti-B antibodies</div>    </li>    <li>      <div>AB - Blood group AB has both A and B antigens but no antibodies, as it would destroy itself.</div>    </li>  </ul>  <p>For further details on the     <a href="te:NHS Business Definitions|tm:ABO System">ABO System</a>, see the NHS website at:     <a href="https://www.nhs.uk/conditions/blood-groups/" target="_blank">Blood groups</a>.  </p>
